INTRODUCTION
Uterine fibroids are noncancerous growths of the uterus that often appear during childbearing years. They aren't associated with an increased risk of uterine cancer and almost never develop into cancer.

RISK FACTORS
1. Age
2. Obesity
3. Family history
4. High blood pressure
5. No history of pregnancy
6. Vitamin D deficiency
7. Food additive consumption
8. Use of soyabean milk

EARLY SYMPTOMS
1. Heavy menstrual bleeding
2. Menstrual periods lasting more than a week
3. Pelvic pressure or pain
4. Frequent urination
5. Difficulty emptying the bladder
6. Constipation
7. Backache or leg pains

HOW TO TAKE CARE OF ONESELF
1. Avoid added salt
2. Limit high-sodium processed and packaged foods
3. Check your blood pressure dail with a home monitor
4. Exercise regularly 
5. Lose weight
6. Avoid or limit alcohol
7. Increase potassium intake
